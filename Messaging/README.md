# Messaging

The verbal brand — language, tone, and canonical copy your team pulls from.

## Subfolder guide

- **`Boilerplate/`** — company description, about-us paragraphs at 50/150/300 words, founder bios.
- **`Messaging-Framework/`** — positioning statement, value propositions, key messages, proof points.
- **`Taglines-and-CTAs/`** — approved taglines, campaign lines, standard CTAs.
- **`Voice-and-Tone/`** — voice guide, tone shifts by channel, do's/don'ts, example phrasings.

## Why this matters

Most brands have a logo file but not a voice file. Then every writer interprets the brand differently and consistency collapses at the copy layer faster than the visual layer.

If you populate only one subfolder in this repo, make it `Voice-and-Tone/`.

## What to include

- One-sentence positioning ("We are the X for Y who need Z")
- Three to five value propositions with proof points
- Tone axes (formal ↔ casual, serious ↔ playful) and where you sit
- Brand vocabulary — preferred terms, forbidden terms
- Before/after examples of on-brand vs. off-brand copy

## What NOT to put here

- Marketing copy for specific campaigns (that's campaign work, not brand)
- Sales scripts (those belong with your sales enablement)
- Legal disclaimers (link out to legal's source of truth)

## Naming

```
brand-messaging-[category]-[name]-v[n].ext
```
