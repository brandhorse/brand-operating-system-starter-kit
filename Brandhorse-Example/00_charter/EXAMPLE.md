# 00_charter — Brandhorse populated example

> The non-negotiables. What we believe, who decides what, and the brand promise downstream work inherits.

---

## truth-set/

Brandhorse believes:

- **Most brand work fails downstream because the upstream chain is missing.** Teams articulate positioning before they've populated evidence. Then "the brand" becomes whatever the last loud meeting decided.
- **Compliance only works against rules that exist.** A do/don't list scattered across Slack threads is not a rule library.
- **Brand is an operating system, not a publication.** A 60-page guidelines PDF is the *output* of brand work. The system is what produced it — and what will produce the next one when the brand evolves.
- **Founders are the constraint.** A brand operating system that requires the founder to interpret every edge case is a bottleneck, not an OS.

Three things competitors don't act like they believe:

1. That evidence beats taste. Most agencies still deliver positioning from intuition + a deck.
2. That anti-voice is half the rule. "Be confident" is meaningless without "and not arrogant — here's where the line is."
3. That brand decisions deserve the same versioning discipline as code.

## decision-rights/

| Decision | Who decides | How decisions get unblocked |
|---|---|---|
| Charter changes | Founder | Charter-level decision; logged in `99_archive/` if superseded |
| Positioning shifts | Founder + Brand Lead | Requires updated evidence in `01_evidence/` |
| Messaging copy | Brand Lead | Standing approval against `04_positioning-and-messaging/claims-library/` |
| Voice rules | Brand Lead | Updated against `05_voice-and-anti-voice/` |
| Rule library entries | Brand Lead + Compliance | Severity-tagged; flagged in compliance runs |
| Approved canon | Founder | Frameworks committed to; not changeable without charter-level decision |
| Campaign briefs | Brand Lead + Marketing Lead | Inherit governance from upstream; brief CAN'T ship without explicit linkage |
| Asset blueprints | Brand Lead + Design Lead | Specs locked; master files versioned |
| Shipped instances | Marketing Lead + Design Lead | Compliance-runs gate publication |

When a decision can't be unblocked at the named level, it escalates to Founder + 24-hour SLA. Anything that escalates twice in a quarter is a signal the rule library is missing a row.

## charter/

**Brandhorse**, in one paragraph:

> Brandhorse turns brand positioning into a governed, repeatable operating system. We help founders and operators move from scattered brand decisions across docs, chats, drives, and decks to a versioned chain of command — evidence → patterns → positioning → voice → rules → canon → briefs → execution — so the brand compounds instead of resetting every time someone new joins the team.

**Mission:** Make brand defensible at the operating-system level, not the deliverable level.

**Operating principles:**

- **Evidence first.** No claim ships without provenance.
- **Anti is half.** Voice without anti-voice is half the rule.
- **Rules over taste.** Taste doesn't scale; rules do.
- **Audit trail by default.** Every brand decision is versioned and traceable.
- **The system, not the document.** We ship operating systems, not 60-page PDFs.

**Brand promise:** Your team stops arguing about brand because the system tells them what's allowed.
