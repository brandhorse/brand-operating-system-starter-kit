# 07_approved-canon — Brandhorse populated example

> The frameworks the brand has committed to. Versioned, governed, and not changeable without a charter-level decision.

---

## storytelling-frameworks/

**Approved framework: "Problem → Insight → Shift"**

The narrative structure every long-form Brandhorse story follows.

1. **Problem** — Open with the operator's specific pain. Concrete, evidence-backed.
2. **Insight** — Reveal what the evidence actually means. The shift in how to see the problem.
3. **Shift** — Show the operating change that follows from the insight.

Used in: blog posts, founder LinkedIn posts, podcast pitches, sales decks.

**Why approved:** This structure showed up in 8 of our 10 highest-engagement posts in 2025. Pattern is real. Charter-level commitment because narrative consistency matters.

## communication-frameworks/

**Approved framework: "Channel-aware message degradation"**

How a single message gets adapted across surfaces while preserving the spine.

| Surface | Length | Voice register | Required elements |
|---|---|---|---|
| Long-form blog | 1500–2500 words | Operator-to-operator, full register | Evidence citations, claims-library tags |
| LinkedIn post | 100–250 words | Operator-to-operator | One concrete example, one claim |
| X thread | 4–8 tweets | Bolder, shorter | One claim per tweet |
| Email subject | <60 chars | Direct | No rhetorical questions |
| Sales-call opener | 1–2 sentences | Conversational | Reference the customer's stated pain |
| Founder DM | <100 words | Personal voice | Customer name, specific reference |

**Why approved:** Without this framework, the same message gets restated in 6 incompatible ways across 6 channels. Compliance can't run against unrendered messages.

## brand-personas/

**Approved persona: Founder voice (Jeff)**

When Jeff posts on LinkedIn, X, or sends a personal email:

- First person, casual register
- Lowercase running heads OK; full lowercase OK
- Can reference Brandhorse but the speaker is Jeff, not the company
- Allowed to be opinionated; encouraged to be wrong sometimes if the wrong-ness is generative

**Approved persona: Brandhorse system voice**

When the OS itself produces copy (compliance messages, in-app notifications, error states):

- Imperative or declarative; never apologetic
- Tells the user what's allowed and what isn't
- Cites the rule ID so the user can investigate

**Approved persona: Brandhorse marketing voice**

When the marketing surface is producing copy on behalf of the company:

- Operator-to-operator register
- Lowercase + italic-heading rule applies
- Never first person plural beyond "we're building Brandhorse" attribution

**Banned personas:**

- "Brandhorse-as-thought-leader" (gets too guru-y)
- "Brandhorse-as-friend" (over-familiar, undermines positioning)
