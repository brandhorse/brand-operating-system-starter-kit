# 03_personas-and-profiles — Brandhorse populated example

> Who the brand is built for, who it's *not* built for, and the people we used to serve but no longer prioritize.

---

## icp/

**Primary ICP: Operator-founder of a 10–75 person brand**

- **Title pattern:** Founder, CEO, or Head of Brand. Hands-on. Still personally responsible for whether a campaign ships clean.
- **Stage:** Past product-market fit. Has a brand that works *but* breaks under team scale.
- **Pain spectrum:** Brand decisions live across 4–7 surfaces (Notion, Slack, Drive, Figma, Frame.io, email). Single-threaded on one designer or copywriter. Compliance is reactive — caught at QA or post-ship, not at brief.
- **Buying trigger:** Someone leaves and takes context with them, OR a campaign ships off-brand and the team can't say why.
- **Budget:** $497 to $20K+ depending on assistance level.
- **Evidence cited:** customer-quotes #1, #2, #3 (in `01_evidence/`).

**Anti-claim:** Brandhorse is *not* for pre-PMF startups. They don't have enough brand decisions to operate against yet.

---

## secondary-personas/

**Secondary 1: Agency owners shipping brand systems as deliverables**

- They're already producing deliverables; we make their deliverables defensible.
- Different motion: they buy us to embed in client work, not to install on themselves.
- Constraint: They want white-label-friendly outputs.

**Secondary 2: Brand-ops / design-ops leaders inside larger orgs**

- 100–500 person companies, often with chief-of-staff or COO sponsorship.
- Buying motion is committee-driven; sales cycle 60–120 days.
- We earn the seat by demonstrating compliance ROI in regulated industries.

---

## anti-personas/

**Anti-persona 1: Solo creators / personal brands.**
Why they're a no: not enough surface area. A single person's brand is their judgment. They don't need an OS — they need a workspace.

**Anti-persona 2: Pre-product startups.**
Why they're a no: no evidence to populate the chain. Brand work that precedes traction is guessing.

**Anti-persona 3: Enterprise (5000+ people).**
Why they're a no for now: procurement complexity exceeds our delivery capacity. Possible future segment, not current.

**Anti-persona 4: "I just need a logo" buyers.**
Why they're a no: they don't have a brand problem; they have a deliverable need. Refer them to a freelancer or 3 Day Website. Not us.
